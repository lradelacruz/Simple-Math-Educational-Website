What is Mathematics?

Mathematics is the discipline that studies the logic of form, number, and arrangement. Math is present in everything we do. It is the foundation for everything in our everyday lives, including mobile devices, ancient and modern architecture, art, money, engineering, and even sports.
Mathematical exploration has always been at the core of all human societies. Mathematical demands emerged as a result of societal desires. Math was used by primitive tribes to measure the angle of the sun and the mechanics of shooting. The greater the complexity of a society, the greater the complexity of its mathematical requirements.



Addition

The process or skill of calculating the total of two or more numbers or amounts.
The addition is taking two or more numbers and adding them together, that is, it is the total sum of 2 or more numbers.



Subtraction

The process of taking a matrix, vector, or other quantity away from another under specific rules to obtain the difference.
To subtract means to take away from a group or a number of things.
When we subtract, the number of things in the group reduce or become less.



Multiplication

one of the four basic operations of arithmetic, gives the result of combining groups of equal sizes.
To multiply means to add equal groups. When we multiply, the number of things in the group increases.



Quotient

method of distributing a group of things into equal parts. It is one of the four basic operations of arithmetic, which gives a fair result of sharing.
